United Nations Measures

Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water

The United Nations has set a goal to conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas, and marine resources for sustainable development. This goal aims to reduce marine pollution, protect marine biodiversity, and promote sustainable fishing practices.

UN Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS)

The UNCLOS is an international treaty that sets out the legal framework for the use of the world's oceans. It aims to promote the conservation and sustainable use of marine resources, and to protect the marine environment.

Read More

UN Environment Programme (UNEP)

The UNEP is a leading global environmental authority that sets the global environmental agenda. It works to address the environmental impacts of human activities on the oceans, including pollution, overfishing, and climate change.

Read More

Global Programme of Action for the Protection of the Marine Environment from Land-based Activities (GPA)

The GPA is a global initiative that aims to reduce the impact of land-based activities on the marine environment. It focuses on reducing pollution from land-based sources, such as agricultural runoff and industrial waste.

Read More

Global Marine Programmes

The UN supports various global marine programmes, such as the International Maritime Organization (IMO) which works to reduce pollution from ships and manage maritime safety.

Read More

International Whaling Commission (IWC)

The International Whaling Commission (IWC), established in 1946, is responsible for regulating whaling and ensuring whale conservation. It imposed a moratorium on commercial whaling in 1986 to protect whale populations.
