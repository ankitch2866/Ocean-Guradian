International Whaling Commission (IWC)

The International Whaling Commission (IWC), established in 1946, is an international body dedicated to the conservation of whale populations and the management of whaling activities worldwide.

  • Conservation of Whales
    Objective: Works to ensure the sustainable conservation of whale species and their habitats.
    Actions: Implement measures to protect and conserve whale populations and their environments.
  • Regulation of Whaling
    Objective: Sets regulations for whaling activities, including catch limits and methods to prevent over-exploitation.
    Actions: Establish and enforce whaling regulations to prevent over-exploitation.
  • Research and Monitoring
    Objective: Conducts and supports scientific research to monitor whale populations and their health.
    Actions: Support research initiatives and monitor whale health and population trends.
  • Whale Sanctuaries
    Objective: Establishes protected areas where commercial whaling is prohibited, such as the Southern Ocean Whale Sanctuary.
    Actions: Designate and manage whale sanctuaries to protect whales from commercial hunting.
  • Cultural and Subsistence Whaling
    Objective: Regulates subsistence whaling for indigenous communities while ensuring it does not threaten whale populations.
    Actions: Oversee and regulate subsistence whaling practices.
  • Moratorium on Commercial Whaling
    Objective: Implemented in 1986, this moratorium prohibits commercial whaling to allow whale populations to recover.
    Actions: Enforce the moratorium to support whale population recovery.
  • Scientific Permits
    Objective: Allows for the issuance of special permits for scientific research, though this has been a subject of controversy.
    Actions: Regulate the issuance of scientific research permits.
  • Conservation Management Plans
    Objective: Develops strategies to address specific threats to whale populations, such as bycatch and habitat degradation.
    Actions: Create and implement management plans to address threats.
  • Global Collaboration
    Objective: Partners with other organizations and countries to strengthen whale conservation efforts and policies.
    Actions: Collaborate with international entities to enhance whale conservation.
  • Membership
    Objective: Composed of member countries that have signed the International Convention for the Regulation of Whaling (ICRW).
    Actions: Facilitate membership and encourage active participation.
  • Decision-Making
    Objective: Decisions are made by consensus or majority vote, with some decisions requiring a three-fourths majority.
    Actions: Ensure fair and transparent decision-making processes.

The IWC plays a vital role in the global efforts to protect whales and ensure the sustainable management of whaling practices.
