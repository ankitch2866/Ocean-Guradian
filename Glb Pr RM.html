Global Programme of Action for the Protection of the Marine Environment from Land-based Activities (GPA)

The Global Programme of Action for the Protection of the Marine Environment from Land-based Activities (GPA), established in 1995, is an initiative under the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) aimed at preventing the degradation of the marine environment from land-based activities.

  • Pollution Reduction
    Objective: Reduce pollution from land-based sources, such as agricultural runoff, sewage, and industrial discharges.
    Actions: Implement measures to control and reduce land-based pollution.
  • Ecosystem Protection
    Objective: Protect marine ecosystems by addressing the root causes of marine degradation.
    Actions: Develop strategies to safeguard and restore marine ecosystems.
  • Sustainable Development
    Objective: Promote sustainable practices to ensure the health and productivity of marine environments.
    Actions: Advocate for and support sustainable development practices.
  • Wastewater Management
    Objective: Implement strategies to reduce the impact of sewage and wastewater on marine environments.
    Actions: Develop and enforce wastewater management policies.
  • Nutrient Management
    Objective: Address the issue of nutrient pollution, particularly from agricultural sources, to prevent harmful algal blooms and dead zones.
    Actions: Implement nutrient management practices and controls.
  • Marine Litter
    Objective: Combat marine litter and plastic pollution through prevention, reduction, and management strategies.
    Actions: Promote waste reduction and recycling programs.
  • Coastal and Marine Ecosystems
    Objective: Protect and restore critical habitats such as coral reefs, mangroves, and seagrasses.
    Actions: Support conservation and restoration projects for vital marine habitats.
  • National Action Plans (NAPs)
    Objective: Encourage countries to develop and implement National Action Plans to address land-based sources of marine pollution.
    Actions: Facilitate the creation and execution of NAPs.
  • Capacity Building
    Objective: Provide training and technical assistance to enhance countries’ abilities to manage land-based marine pollution.
    Actions: Offer capacity-building programs and resources.
  • Partnerships and Collaboration
    Objective: Foster partnerships between governments, NGOs, and the private sector to tackle marine pollution.
    Actions: Develop and strengthen collaborative initiatives.
  • Public Awareness and Education
    Objective: Raise awareness about the impacts of land-based activities on the marine environment and promote sustainable practices.
    Actions: Conduct awareness campaigns and educational programs.

The GPA is a critical initiative for mitigating the impacts of land-based activities on the marine environment, ensuring the protection and sustainable use of the world’s oceans.
