International Whaling Commission (IWC)

The International Whaling Commission (IWC), established in 1946 under the International Convention for the Regulation of Whaling (ICRW), is dedicated to conserving whale populations and managing whaling activities.

  • Conservation of Whales
    Objective: Ensures sustainable conservation of whale species worldwide.
    Actions: Implement measures to protect and conserve whale populations.
  • Regulation of Whaling
    Objective: Sets regulations for whaling, including catch limits and whaling methods.
    Actions: Enforce catch limits and ensure humane whaling practices.
  • Research and Monitoring
    Objective: Conducts and supports scientific research on whale populations.
    Actions: Monitor whale populations and conduct research to inform conservation efforts.
  • Sanctuaries
    Objective: Establishes whale sanctuaries where commercial whaling is prohibited.
    Actions: Designate and manage sanctuaries to protect whales from commercial hunting.
  • Cultural and Subsistence Whaling
    Objective: Recognizes and regulates subsistence whaling for indigenous communities.
    Actions: Ensure sustainable practices for indigenous subsistence whaling.
  • Moratorium on Commercial Whaling
    Objective: In effect since 1986 to allow whale populations to recover.
    Actions: Maintain the moratorium to support whale population recovery.
  • Scientific Permits
    Objective: Allows special permits for scientific research, though controversial.
    Actions: Issue and regulate permits for scientific research on whales.
  • Conservation Management Plans
    Objective: Develops plans to mitigate threats to specific whale populations.
    Actions: Create and implement management plans to address threats.
  • Collaborative Efforts
    Objective: Partners with other organizations to enhance whale conservation.
    Actions: Collaborate with international organizations to strengthen conservation efforts.
  • Membership
    Objective: Composed of member countries that have signed the ICRW.
    Actions: Facilitate membership and encourage participation in IWC activities.
  • Decision-Making
    Objective: Decisions are made by consensus or majority vote, with some requiring a three-fourths majority.
    Actions: Ensure transparent and democratic decision-making processes.

The IWC plays a crucial role in global whale conservation efforts, ensuring the protection of whale species for future generations.
